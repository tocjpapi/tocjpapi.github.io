Drama Club—(05);

(24’).

Web Design & Development

‘Drama Club’ is a simple one—pager advertisement website made for the Drama Club at my University.

Alignment Break.

Problem;

There was something missing. The drama club’s presence lived only in passing posters and whispered excitement. They wondered: what if their story had a stage online? This wasn’t just about a website. It was about honouring emotion, craft, and connection—designing a space as considered and expressive as the performances themselves.

Role;

I became both designer and developer. As designer, I shaped emotion into interface—every line, every motion, considered. As developer, I built the invisible: a structure that felt effortless, yet held everything together. My role was to listen, translate, and craft something that felt less like a website, and more like presence.

Framed Shots;

A rather lovely collection containing and exploring my best shots for the project. It’s basically a display of the parts I could very often look back on and say, “I’m proud of this.”
